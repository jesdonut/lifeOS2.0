Privacy

We don't have your data.

LifeOS is built so that your personal information never reaches us. There is no account, no server, no database on our end. This page explains where your data actually goes.

The short version: everything stays on your device. We collect nothing. We store nothing. We see nothing.

How your data is stored

When you use LifeOS, all your information — your name, dates, finances, health data, notes — is saved to your browser's localStorage. This is a storage area that lives entirely on your device, like a file on your computer. It is not sent anywhere.

If you clear your browser data, your LifeOS data clears with it. That's why we give you the option to export a backup file. That file also stays on your device — we never see it.

Apple Health import

LifeOS can help you import data from Apple Health. Here is exactly how that works:

1. You export your data from the Health app on your iPhone. Apple gives you a zip file that goes directly to your device — not to us.

2. You open that file in LifeOS. The conversion happens entirely in your browser. The file is read locally, processed locally, and the result is saved to your localStorage.

The file never leaves your device. We never receive it, never touch it, never store it.

Cookies and tracking

LifeOS uses no cookies, no analytics, no tracking scripts, no third-party services that collect data about you. There is nothing to opt out of.

The only external resource loaded on the landing page is the Google Fonts icon library (Material Symbols), which is used for icons. Google may log that request. If that concerns you, the app itself functions without it.

Your backup file

You can export your data as a JSON file at any time. This file contains everything you've entered. You can keep it, move it to another device, or import it back into LifeOS. It is your file — we never see it or receive a copy.

Open source

LifeOS is open source. You can read every line of code and verify these claims yourself. If you find something that contradicts what's written here, please open an issue on GitHub.

Questions

If you have any questions about privacy, you can reach out via GitHub.
